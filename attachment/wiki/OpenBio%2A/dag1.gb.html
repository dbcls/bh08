96            Summary: Dystroglycan is a laminin binding component of the
97            dystrophin-glycoprotein complex which provides a linkage between
98            the subsarcolemmal cytoskeleton and the extracellular matrix.
99            Dystroglycan 1 is a candidate gene for the site of the mutation in
100            autosomal recessive muscular dystrophies. The dramatic reduction of
101            dystroglycan 1 in Duchenne muscular dystrophy leads to a loss of
102            linkage between the sarcolemma and extracellular matrix, rendering
103            muscle fibers more susceptible to necrosis. Dystroglycan also
104            functions as dual receptor for agrin and laminin-2 in the Schwann
105            cell membrane. The muscle and nonmuscle isoforms of dystroglycan
106            differ by carbohydrate moieties but not protein sequence.
